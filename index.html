Introduction

Before diving into the components of the email validation regular expression, it's essential to understand the need for such patterns. In web development, validating user input, especially email addresses, is a common task. This tutorial will guide you through creating a regex for this purpose.

Component 1 - Username

The username is the first part of an email address. It allows for alphanumeric characters (both uppercase and lowercase) and underscores. The regular expression for the username component is [a-zA-Z0-9_]+. This means one or more characters that are either letters (both cases), digits, or underscores.

Component 2 - @ Symbol

The @ symbol is a mandatory separator between the username and the domain in an email address. In the regular expression, simply including the @ symbol ensures its presence. The regular expression snippet is @.

Component 3 - Domain

The domain is the second part of an email address, following the @ symbol. It allows for alphanumeric characters (both uppercase and lowercase) and hyphens. The regular expression for the domain component is [a-zA-Z0-9-]+. This means one or more characters that are either letters (both cases), digits, or hyphens.

Conclusion
